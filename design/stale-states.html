Fade vs Stale — not the same thing

A user asked: “how do we tell a fade-out apart from stale data?” Side by side, the answer is obvious. A fade keeps full colour and moves; Stale is desaturated, dead still, and carries a quiet age line beneath the pill.

Greyed-out mustard, perfectly still. Desaturated and dimmed, no motion at all, with a quiet stale · updated 6m ago line blended beneath the pill. Muted, not gone.

The rule: a fade keeps full colour and moves (transient); stale is desaturated, steady, and wears a quiet age sub-label. The sub-label + desaturation + stillness are the tells.
