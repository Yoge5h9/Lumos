Lumos interaction prototypes
felt in the browser · pre-Swift

Two interactions, tuned by feel.

Lumos should be present, not pushy. These live prototypes let you feel the two moments that decide that — how the notch glow rests and wakes, and how the menu-bar dot separates a glance from a control. Nothing here is final; every knob is here to be moved.

1 Adaptive brightness · the Halo

Dim at rest, bright on attention

An ambient glow at full strength all day is noise. So the Halo rests dim — barely there — then blooms bright on hover or fresh activity, holds, and eases back down. Hover the notch, or drive it with the buttons, and tune the resting floor, the peak, the hold and the fade until it feels right.

2 The menu-bar LED

Hover to glance, click to control

Hover the dot and the exact numbers appear in a calm HUD, right where your eye already is — no click, no window. Click the same dot and it becomes the control surface: toggles, notifications, updates. One is information, the other is action — and they never fight for the screen.
